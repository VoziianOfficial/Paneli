1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that may be stored on a user’s browser when visiting a website. Similar technologies may include localStorage, sessionStorage, pixels, scripts, or device identifiers.

These technologies can help a website remember choices, understand basic usage, improve security, and provide a smoother browsing experience.

2. How Paneli may use cookies and storage

Paneli may use cookies or browser storage for basic website operation, policy banner preferences, form usability, analytics, performance, and security.

  • Remembering whether a user accepted or declined the policy banner.
  • Supporting navigation, page functionality, and user preferences.
  • Understanding general website usage and page performance.
  • Helping protect the site against misuse, spam, or technical issues.
  • Improving the clarity and usability of the provider-matching experience.
Policy banner storage

The website may store a simple localStorage value so the policy banner does not reappear on every page after a user makes a choice.

3. Types of cookies or storage that may be used

Essential functionality

These technologies may be needed for basic site navigation, policy choices, security, accessibility, or technical operation.

Preference storage

These may remember choices such as accepting or declining the policy banner.

Analytics or performance

These may help understand general site interaction, page performance, and areas where the website can be improved.

Communication or form support

These may support contact forms, selected service choices, or request-related interactions.

4. Third-party tools

The website may use third-party tools for hosting, analytics, performance, security, forms, or communication. These tools may use their own cookies or similar technologies according to their own policies.

Paneli does not control third-party cookie practices. Users should review third-party policies where applicable.

5. User choices

Users can manage cookies and storage through browser settings. Depending on the browser, users may be able to block, delete, or limit cookies and stored data.

  • Use browser settings to delete cookies or site data.
  • Use browser settings to block certain cookies.
  • Use private browsing modes where available.
  • Decline optional policy banner choices where offered.

Blocking some cookies or storage may affect website functionality, saved preferences, or form behavior.

6. Relationship to provider matching

Cookies and browser storage do not create a contractor relationship and do not hire any provider. They are used to support site functionality and user experience.

Providers connected through the platform are independent. Homeowners should verify licensing, insurance, estimates, timelines, warranties, and project terms directly.
